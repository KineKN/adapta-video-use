# Ad House Funnels

## Overview

The Adapta corpus is not one generic ad format.

A few recurring funnel families appear many times. If you identify the family early, you stop making the main mistake: cleaning the cut while deleting the specific thing that sells.

## Use When

- the ad is from the Adapta house style
- you already identified the commercial arc, but need to know what details are non-negotiable
- multiple retakes say the same thing with different emphasis
- you need to decide whether a strange fragment is noise or part of the sales mechanism

## Rule Zero

Do not compress a house funnel into generic language.

`nove IAs premium` is not the same as `varias IAs`.

`PDF gabarito` is not the same as `um documento`.

`empresa nativa de IA` is not the same as `melhorar a empresa com IA`.

Also, classify by the dominant editorial driver, not just by noun frequency.

A whisper/scarcity ad can still sell `nove IAs premium`, but the operative funnel for editing may still be `Whisper / Secret / Fast Click CTA`.

## Funnel Families

### 1. Nine Premium AIs / Price Stack / Free-for-a-Year

Typical pattern:

`price anchor -> list of premium tools -> stacked monthly cost -> surprising condition -> CTA`

Usually preserve:

- names of the flagship tools
- quantity claim: `9` or `nove das melhores`
- stacked cost logic: `20 a 30 dolares`, `120 a 150 reais`, `500 reais por mes`
- offer framing: `um ano`, `premium`, `de graca`
- the single condition

Common damage to avoid:

- removing the cost anchor
- dropping named tools and leaving only category words
- flattening the surprise around the condition
- softening urgency in the CTA

### 2. PDF Gabarito / Upload This PDF

Typical pattern:

`curiosity or authority seed -> attach PDF -> IA responds much better -> scientific justification -> free download CTA`

Usually preserve:

- `PDF` as the object, not a generic file
- one-line mechanism: upload/attach and the IA improves
- scientific proof details when present: `4 mil artigos`, `20 tecnicas`, `uma pagina`, `600 palavras`
- ease claim: user does not need to read it
- acquisition path: click, leave email, receive PDF

Common damage to avoid:

- cutting the authority seed when it is the hook
- removing the "nao precisa ler" relief
- keeping side banter after the mechanism is already clear

### 3. Cancel ChatGPT / Better Bundle Tool

Typical pattern:

`I cancelled ChatGPT -> why one model is not enough -> multiple premium models together -> task-by-task comparison -> low-risk trial CTA`

Usually preserve:

- the cancellation hook
- named models and why each matters
- separate-vs-bundle price comparison
- trial or guarantee window
- timing or scarcity argument around price increase

Common damage to avoid:

- weakening the anti-ChatGPT hook
- removing the per-model use cases that justify the bundle
- duplicating the CTA after the guarantee block

### 4. Ranking / Adapta Pass as #1

Typical pattern:

`external ranking -> quick judgments on tools -> Adapta Pass absorbs the winners -> business utility -> first-place payoff`

Usually preserve:

- the existence of the ranking
- at least a few concrete rank judgments
- why Adapta Pass is different: `nao e so uma IA`
- business-use outcomes: systems, automation, sharing with team
- the first-place conclusion

Common damage to avoid:

- cutting too many ranked examples so the ranking loses meaning
- replacing `primeiro lugar` or `excelente` payoff with vague praise
- trusting a weak corpus final if it is visibly corrupted

### 5. Empresa Nativa de IA / B2B Qualification

Typical pattern:

`company-size qualifier -> authority claim -> B2B transformation promise -> special program -> CTA`

Usually preserve:

- the qualifier threshold, such as `2 milhoes por ano`
- `empresa nativa de IA`
- authority proof: scale, leadership, platform claim
- special-program positioning

Common damage to avoid:

- deleting the qualifier and making the ad broad
- replacing the transformation concept with generic productivity talk

### 6. Whisper / Secret / Fast Click CTA

Typical pattern:

`come closer secret hook -> surprising free claim -> one condition -> scarcity whisper -> urgent CTA`

Usually preserve:

- the intimate hook
- the exact free claim
- the single-condition turn
- the whisper/secret framing
- the final urgent push, including fragments like `Vai, clica!`

Common damage to avoid:

- normalizing the whisper hook into plain speech
- removing the final rhythmic imperative

### 7. Anti-Free vs Premium / Status Threat

Typical pattern:

`status threat -> free tool humiliation -> evidence that free is degraded -> premium is necessary -> rescue offer -> gated CTA`

Usually preserve:

- the status-threat opener
- the humiliation wording if it is intentional direct-response pressure
- the concrete degradation proof: peak hours, lower intelligence, generic output
- the bridge from `premium is necessary` to the actual offer
- the specific rescue offer: quantity, premium access, one year, platform, zero subscription cost
- the gate: condition or prerequisite

Common damage to avoid:

- stopping at the problem and never landing the real offer
- keeping the pressure but deleting the rescue mechanism
- softening the aggressive language so much that the ad loses its edge

### 8. Authority Curiosity / Watch And Report Back

Typical pattern:

`personal confession -> authority borrowed from known figure -> unusual mechanism or technique stack -> request for validation -> hidden-access CTA`

Usually preserve:

- the personal confession or request for help
- the authority anchor, such as `Renato Cariani`
- the named mechanisms and proof details
- the reason the speaker is asking the viewer to watch too
- the hidden-access turn: not on YouTube, not public, click to watch

Common damage to avoid:

- normalizing the opener into a generic recommendation
- compressing named techniques into vague productivity claims
- losing the “watch and tell me if I’m crazy” loop that powers the CTA

### 9. Summit Event / Sold-Out Qualification

Typical pattern:

`scarcity hook -> prior sold-out proof -> bigger current edition -> anti-event differentiation -> qualification/filter -> CTA`

Usually preserve:

- sold-out hook
- chronology and scale proof
- anti-event turn
- the qualification logic: not everyone gets in, the viewer must earn a place
- the CTA path: watch the video, understand the event, get the spot

Common damage to avoid:

- replacing the anti-event angle with generic event hype
- deleting the qualification/filter turn
- flattening the chronology into an unconvincing summary

### 10. Medico Nativo de IA / Transplante de IA

Typical pattern:

`specialist authority -> native-IA identity shift -> consultorio mechanism -> time/revenue payoff -> CTA`

Usually preserve:

- the medical or specialty qualifier
- the identity shift: `medico nativo de IA` or `transplante de IA`
- concrete consultorio mechanisms: prontuario, anamnese, agenda, WhatsApp, follow-up
- the time, capacity, or revenue payoff
- the named authority, especially `Paulo Muzy`, when present

Common damage to avoid:

- replacing the clinical mechanism with generic productivity talk
- deleting the identity shift and leaving only broad AI hype
- removing the specialist proof that makes the offer credible

### 11. Desafio Programa de IA

Typical pattern:

`challenge invitation -> day-by-day promise -> operational payoff -> urgency to join -> CTA`

Usually preserve:

- the challenge mechanic itself
- who the challenge is for
- the daily or task-by-task progression
- the concrete operational payoff
- the join path: button, group, invite, or start date

Common damage to avoid:

- flattening the challenge into generic AI education
- cutting the target audience qualifier
- deleting the day-by-day structure that makes the offer tangible

### 12. Trial Update Pack / 30-Day Test

Typical pattern:

`update or launch hook -> biggest pack/new platform -> risk-free test -> bonus/proof -> CTA`

Usually preserve:

- the 30-day or risk-free test logic
- the update-pack or new-platform novelty
- concrete proof: new tools, new features, bonuses, presents
- the no-risk or refund path

Common damage to avoid:

- keeping only broad value claims and deleting the test mechanic
- losing the novelty that justifies the offer
- cutting the concrete CTA into the trial

## Selection Rules

- If two takes say the same funnel detail, keep the stronger performance, not the flatter wording.
- If a detail appears repeatedly across the family, treat it as load-bearing until proven otherwise.
- If the corpus `Final.txt` looks weak, corrupted, or obviously incomplete, prefer the better commercial logic from the bruto.

## Quick Classification

Ask:

- Is the sale built on stacked AI value, a PDF mechanism, a ranking, a cancellation contrast, a B2B qualifier, or a whispered secret?
- Which exact nouns and numbers make this funnel believable?
- Which line creates the turn into the CTA?
- Is this a hybrid where the offer family is one thing but the delivery frame is another?

If the classifier comes back `medium`, `low`, or `unknown`, do not force a family reading just because one noun matched.
In those cases, preserve the raw commercial arc first and treat the funnel label as a tentative guide.

If the ad is flagged as hybrid, distinguish:

- benign overlap: common adjacency between neighboring house funnels
- review overlap: the pair is common, but wording-level pull should be cautious
- dangerous overlap: the pair often drifts into structural or wording mistakes if treated casually

## Pass Criteria

A correct cut should still sound like the same specific ad family, not a generic direct-response paraphrase.
