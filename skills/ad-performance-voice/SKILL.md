# Ad Performance Voice

## Overview

Not every irregular fragment is noise.

In this corpus, some fragments sound rough because they are sales performance, not because they are mistakes.

## Use When

- the speaker uses short emphatic bursts
- the transcript has unusual fragments that may still be intentional
- two candidate edits have the same meaning but different intensity
- the cut feels semantically correct but commercially flatter

## Preserve When It Sells

Keep fragments that add:

- urgency
- surprise
- authority
- intimacy
- rhythm
- direct-response energy

Examples already observed in the corpus:

- `Oitavo!`
- `Free.`
- `Exato.`
- `Vai, clica!`
- `Chega mais perto`
- `Eu juro`
- `Pula esse anuncio`
- `ferramenta de frouxo`
- `Sem exagero.`
- `Eu aposto que voce vai voltar concordando comigo.`

## Cut When It Is Noise

Cut fragments that are obviously:

- crew coordination
- aborted setup
- self-correction with no sales value
- filler before a cleaner intended version
- broken audio contamination

## Selection Rule

If two lines carry the same sales meaning:

- prefer the one with stronger persuasion
- unless that stronger line is contaminated by set noise, cough, overlap, or broken syntax

## Warning Signs Of Over-Normalization

- the ad still makes sense, but sounds safer
- the CTA loses force
- the hook loses personality
- the speaker sounds like written copy instead of spoken persuasion
- a personal confession turns into a neutral recommendation
- a harsh status-threat ad becomes merely explanatory

## QC Questions

- Did the cut preserve the speaker's selling rhythm?
- Did any short burst get removed only because it looked awkward on the page?
- Did the CTA become flatter than the best source take?

## Pass Criteria

The final should sound intentional, persuasive, and alive, not merely clean.
